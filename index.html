FAQ

Questions, Answered Straight.

How much does TV mounting cost in Honolulu?

It depends on TV size, wall type, and whether cables go inside the wall — but you'll get one flat, written price before we start, and it won't change unless the job does. Call, text, or use the 60-second quote form and you'll have a number within the hour during business hours.

Can you mount a TV on concrete walls?

Yes — concrete is our home turf. Most Oahu condos and townhomes are poured concrete, so we carry masonry bits and anchors rated well beyond your TV's weight.

Can you hide the cables inside the wall?

Usually, yes. We check for studs, fire blocks, and outlets first. Where in-wall isn't possible (like solid concrete), we use a slim paintable raceway so nothing dangles.

Do I need to buy the mount, or do you bring one?

Either. If you have one, we'll use it. If not, tell us your TV size and wall and we'll bring the right mount and include it in your flat quote.

How fast can you come? Do you do same-day?

Often, yes — especially if you reach out in the morning. Otherwise it's usually next-day. Mon–Sat, 8am–7pm, all of Oahu.

Are you licensed and insured? What if something gets damaged?

Happy Max Handyman Service LLC is a licensed and insured Hawaii company. In the rare case something goes wrong, insurance covers it — you're never left holding the bill. And our work is backed by a 12-month workmanship guarantee.

Who actually shows up at my house?

Max — the owner, the person you talked to, the name on the company. Not a rotating crew of strangers.

Will there be a mess afterward?

No. Drop cloths before we drill, vacuum after, and all packaging and old hardware leave with us.

What if the price changes once you see the job?

It doesn't — that's the point of a flat quote. If we open a wall and find a genuine surprise, we stop, show you, and agree on a new price before continuing. You're never billed for work you didn't approve.

What areas of Oahu do you serve, and what else do you fix?

All of it — Honolulu, Waikiki, Kakaʻako, Kailua, Kaneohe, Pearl City, Aiea, Kapolei, Ewa Beach, Mililani, and up to the North Shore. Beyond TVs: furniture assembly, ceiling fan and light swaps, drywall repair, doors and locks, shelving, and the small-repair list.
